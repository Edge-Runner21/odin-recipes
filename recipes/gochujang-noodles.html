Spicy Korean Gochujang Noodles

This spicy Korean dish is great on those cold evenings when you need a nice hot meal to warm you up. The Gochujang chilli paste gives this dish a nice spicy kick, while the sesame oil gives a sublte nutty taste and the spring onions add a nice fragrant chrunch.

This tastey meal can be ready to eat in as little as fifthteen minutes, so its great to make when it's been a long day and you don't feel like standing over the stove for hours.

Ingredients

  • 200g udon noodles (or noodles of you choice)
  • 2 spring onions
  • 1/2 tbsp vegtable oil
  • 100g ground pork (or ground beef)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Handful of greens
  • Generous pinch of toasted sesame seeds, to garnish
  • 1 tbsp gochujang
  • 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp oyster sauce (or fish sauce)
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru
  • 1/2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp water

Steps

  1. Make the sauce by adding the following ingredients into a bowl and mixing together
  2. 1/2 tbsp sugar
  3. 1 tbsp gochugaru
  4. 1 tbsp gochujang
  5. 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  6. 1/2 tbsp oyster sauce (or fish sauce)
  7. 1 tbsp mirin
  8. 2 tbsp water
  9. 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  10. Set the sauce aside.
  11. Bring a pot of water to the boil.
  12. Add your chosen noodles to pot and cook acroding to packet instructions.
  13. Once cooked, drain the noodles and set aside.
  14. Thinly slice the spring onions and seperate the greens from the whites.
  15. In a wok (or pan) add the vegtable oil and heat it over medium-high heat.
  16. Once the oil is heated add the white parts of the spring onions, ground pork (or ground beef) and salt and peper to taste.
  17. Saulte for 2 minutes or untill no longer pink.
  18. Add the drained noodles and sauce. Cook for 2 minutes or until the noodles are nicely coated.
  19. Plate up and garnish with greens, toasted sesame seeds and the green parts of the spring onions.
  20. Enjoy.
