Gochujang noodles

Description:

This spicy Korean dish is great on those cold evenings when you need a nice hot meal to warm you up. The Gochujang chilli paste gives this dish a nice spicy kick, while the sesame oil gives a subtle nutty taste and the spring onions add a nice fragrant crunch.

This tasty meal can be ready to eat in as little as fifteen minutes, so its great to make when it's been a long day and you don't feel like standing over the stove for hours.

Ingredients:

  • 200g udon noodles (or noodles of you choice)
  • 2 spring onions
  • 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 100g ground pork (or ground beef)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Handful of greens
  • Generous pinch of toasted sesame seeds, to garnish

Sauce:

  • 1 tbsp gochujang
  • 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp oyster sauce (or fish sauce)
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru
  • 1/2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp water

Steps:

  1. Make the sauce: In a small bowl mix together the gochujang, gochugaru, sugar, soy sauce, oyster sauce, mirin, sesame oil and water.
  2. Bring a pot of water to the boil, add your chosen noodles to pot and cook according to packet instructions. Once cooked, drain the noodles and set aside.
  3. Thinly slice the spring onions and separate the greens from the whites.
  4. In a wok (or pan) add the vegetable oil and heat it over medium-high heat. Once the oil is heated add the white parts of the spring onions, ground pork and salt and pepper to taste. Saute for 2 minutes or until no longer pink.
  5. Add the drained noodles and sauce. Stir-fry for 2 minutes or until the noodles are nicely coated.
  6. Plate up and garnish with greens, toasted sesame seeds and the green parts of the spring onions.
  7. Enjoy.
